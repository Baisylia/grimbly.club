Lethal company is a multiplayer comedy/horror roguelike game where you venture into
facilities on different planets to collect scrap for the company, avoiding monsters
and traps on the way. You have 3 days to meet a scrap quota, which increases for the
next 3 days when met. You can use the money earnt from meeting quotas to buy tools,
better planets, and cosmetics, but if the quota isn't met your team is fired.

It has quickly become one of my favourite multiplayer games of all time, its perfect
blend of horror and humour always making for a great experience, even for a wimp like
me that usually doesn't enjoy horror games.

The gameplay loop is very addicting, constantly forcing yourself into dangerous
situations to avoid not meeting the quota, and to me at least this loop is extremely
replayable. This combined with the proximity chat creates unique multiplayer
interactions that often start with everybody goofing off, and end with one lone soul
attempting to escape. 		Replayability: 5

I don't need to explain what Minecraft is, I have no doubt that you already know.
It's the sandbox game that became the best selling game of all time, beating GTA 5
and becoming the only game to sell over 300,000,000 copies of itself.

version on my iPad, and then to the Xbox 360. Eventually I got Java edition, which has
since been my primary version of the game. I played the game when it was new and
exciting, when it was considered cringey, when it had its resurgence, and to this day.

Currently the trend of the Minecraft community is to hate on Mojang, for being lazy,
or about the mob vote, or for any small change they make to features like redstone.
I find it to be quite disappointing that so many feel the need to hate on developers
that update their 15 year old game for free. Especially when these types of people

The Stanley Parable is a choose-your-own-adventure type comedy game that explores
themes such as choice in games and the relationship between player and creator.
The game has a lot of endings, many of which contradict eachother, so it's hard to
say whether or not the game truly has a main storyline. It walks the line between
satire and sincere, in a perfect balance that shouldn't be as fun as it is.

In 2022, The Stanley Parable: Ultra Deluxe was released, as an expansion and
re-imagining of the original game. This version builds upon the foundations of the

fish. The game has a wonderful atmosphere that's great for just sitting around with
your friends, and an art style that I can't get enough of. It's a fairly simple game,
but it feels quite polished, and I particularly appreciate the available character
customisation options and queer representation.

This game is one of the only games I've played where I haven't felt the need to
