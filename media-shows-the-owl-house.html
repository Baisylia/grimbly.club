The Owl House


I've fixated on a lot of things, but I think I can safely say that The Owl House (TOH) is my favourite show.

There really just isn't anything else like it, I mean can you, valued visitor, name another show where the main character is a well-written bisexual and gender non-conforming young girl with ADHD? Or a show that heavily features a disabled bisexual middle-aged woman as a fully fleshed out character? Usually finding just one of those traits in a well written character is difficult enough.

But even excluding the amazing representation, TOH is just such a good watch. It has some pacing issues of course, thanks to Disney cancelling the show and turning the final season into three specials, but even then I think that TOH's finale is one of the best out of the shows I enjoy.

And what is a TOH discussion without mention of Lumity, the best relationship I've seen in a cartoon maybe ever, and a gay one at that! Luz and Amity are fantastic characters and their relationship is a great example for kids and adults alike, and I mean for straight relationships too.

I love the characters of course but their world is just as fun. I really enjoy the magic system, and personally I would study the bard, plant, and abomination tracks. I also find the environments to be really beautiful, I would unironically hang up some of the establishing shots as pictures in my room.

I first got into TOH during production of its second season, and unfortunately got spoiled for season one's ending. Luckily the show was so good that I still loved it anyway, and it was awesome to be there for the episode releases, even if I wasn't active in any communities back then.

It was sad to see the show end, but I became resigned to the fact that we would never get anything else TOH with how Disney treated it and with Dana moving to work at Glitch. That is until The Long Lived King was announced just after I started hyperfixating on it again in December 2025. I still can't believe it now. Long live King indeed, and long live TOH.
